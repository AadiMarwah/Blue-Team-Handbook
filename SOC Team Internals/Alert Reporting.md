#Team_Internals
This is the process of writing detailed reports on alerts, so if escalation is required then the L2 analyst can come up to speed quickly.

---

After triaging has been done, some alerts need in-depth and thorough analysis . These complex alerts which cannot be resolved by the L1 analyst are then escalated to the L2 analyst for review.

The detailed reports that are written help the L2 analyst to come up to speed quickly without wasting time on finding the basic information needed. This is what makes writing detailed reports so essential. 

Now when writing reports there are some good practices or ways that can be followed. One of then is if following the approach of five W's. These W's are as follows -
1. Who (Information on who was attacked/affected)
2. What (Actions taken or event of sequence's that occurred)
3. When (Time-frame of the attack)
4. Where (On which system the attack took place, at what file location,etc.)
5. Why (Main purpose of taking such steps.<mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">Ex - malware was sent to extract data from administrator account</mark>)

By making reports based on the above parameters,the report becomes extremely good for the SOC L2 analyst as he now does not have waste his time by looking for the information needed to start the analysis .
